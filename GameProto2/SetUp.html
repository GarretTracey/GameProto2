Story Set-Up

Director Grizzly: Greetings, Agents. We have a priority one situation: We’ve lost track of Agent X. Agent X is one of our best agents, and if a problem is dangerous enough that they couldn’t handle it, it could be a threat to the entire multiverse. Because Agent X uniquely exists in multiple different dimensions at once, locating them conventionally is impossible. You have all assembled a series of objects that are linked to Agent X across worlds, which should be enough to give us some idea of what’s going on. Doctor Polybius will guide you through the process. She’s one of our foremost experts on dimensional resonance links.

Doctor Polybius: What you have may seem like mundane objects, but they are all linked to something in another world. By using this connection, we can use your objects to glean information about what happened to Agent X. Keep your objects secret for now, revealing them could upset the connection.

The goal of the game is not to correctly guess the mundane objects that the other players brought, but rather to imagine what strange and mysterious objects could exist based on the descriptions and sounds they make. When other players describe an object to you, imagine the context they use them in. Which of the 5 senses are involved in the experience - smell, taste, touch, vision, hearing? What types of actions can these objects be used for, how often are they used, would you use them alone or with two or more people? Making analogies between these objects with other things can also be a very useful strategy. Remember... What may seem mundane in your world might be magical elsewhere!
